About Me

Background & Interests

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Bradford, where I develop algorithms and computational frameworks at the intersection of machine learning, high-performance computing, and data science. My work focuses on making large-scale scientific simulations faster, more accurate, and more accessible.

My Ph.D. at Brandenburd University of Technology (Computational & Mathematical Engineering, 2021) explored experimental analysis and fluid dynamics characterisation of micro-processes involved in pelleting flocculation. I am passionate about bridging the gap between theoretical computational methods and practical real-world impact.

Outside of research, I mentor undergraduates in numerical simulations and data science, contribute to open-source scientific computing packages, and occasionally write on my blog about topics ranging from numerical methods to reproducible research practices.

Research Interests

Core areas of focus and expertise

🧠

Scientific Machine Learning

Developing physics-informed neural networks and operator learning methods to accelerate PDE-constrained simulations in fluid dynamics, materials science, and climate modeling.

⚡

High-Performance Computing

Designing parallel and distributed algorithms for GPU clusters, with a focus on sparse linear algebra, domain decomposition, and scalable data pipelines for exascale computing.

📊

Bayesian Uncertainty Quantification

Applying Bayesian inference, Monte Carlo methods, and Gaussian processes to quantify uncertainty in computational simulations and observational data analyses.

🌐

Graph Neural Networks

Using GNNs and geometric deep learning to model structured scientific data such as molecular graphs, simulation meshes, and knowledge graphs in biomedical applications.

🔬

Computational Biology

Applying data-driven methods to protein structure prediction, single-cell RNA sequencing analysis, and computational drug discovery pipelines.

🔁

Reproducible Research

Building tools and best practices for reproducible, open, and FAIR computational science, including containerization, workflow management, and automated benchmarking.
